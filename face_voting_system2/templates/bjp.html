History of Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP)

Origins (Before BJP Formation)

The roots of BJP go back to 1951 with the formation of Bharatiya Jana Sangh by Syama Prasad Mukherjee. It was inspired by RSS ideology focusing on cultural nationalism.

1977 – Janata Party Phase

After the Emergency (1975–77), several opposition parties merged to form the Janata Party, which came to power in 1977. However, internal conflicts led to its collapse.

1980 – Formation of BJP

BJP was founded in 1980 by Atal Bihari Vajpayee and Lal Krishna Advani. Initially, it followed Gandhian Socialism.

Rise to Power (1990s)

  • Ram Janmabhoomi Movement boosted popularity
  • 1996: First government (short-lived)
  • 1998: Stable government under Vajpayee
  • Pokhran-II nuclear tests

2004–2014: Opposition Phase

BJP lost power in 2004. Congress-led UPA ruled India while BJP rebuilt its strategy.

2014–Present: Modi Era

  • 2014: Narendra Modi led BJP to majority
  • 2019: Re-elected with bigger majority
  • Major policies: GST, Demonetization, Article 370 removal

Ideology of BJP

  • Cultural nationalism (Hindutva)
  • Strong central government
  • Economic development
  • National security
